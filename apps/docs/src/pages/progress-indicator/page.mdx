Progress indicators express an unspecified wait time or display the duration of a
process — a rounded active indicator separated from its track by a small gap.
Determinate indicators show how much of a process has completed; indeterminate
indicators (omit `value`) express an unspecified wait time.

## Linear

```tsx
<LinearProgress value={42} aria-label="Downloading" />
```

<LinearProgressDeterminate />

A small stop indicator marks the end of the track in determinate mode. Pass `wavy` for
the wavy variant — the active indicator amplitude ramps up just after the start and back
down just before the end, flattening to a straight line at both ends.

Omit `value` (or pass `null`) for the indeterminate animation — two independent bars
travel along the track with no fixed track or stop indicator shown.

<LinearProgressIndeterminate />

## Circular

```tsx
<CircularProgress value={42} aria-label="Downloading" />
```

<CircularProgressDeterminate />

Indeterminate circular progress spins continuously while its arc grows and shrinks,
with no track visible. `wavy` morphs the active indicator between a plain ring and a
many-pointed wavy star as its amplitude ramps.

<CircularProgressIndeterminate />

## Accessibility

Both components render `role="progressbar"` (via Base UI's `Progress.Root`) with
`aria-valuemin`/`aria-valuemax`/`aria-valuenow` wired automatically from `value`/`max`;
omitting `value` marks the indicator indeterminate for assistive tech. Pass `aria-label`
to describe what's in progress.
